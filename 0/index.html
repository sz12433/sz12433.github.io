Part 1: Selfie: The Wrong Way vs. The Right Way

The close-up picture results in distortion because the camera is too close to the subject, exaggerating facial features due to perspective distortion. Stepping back and zooming in helps maintain a natural proportion and minimizes distortion, leading to a more accurate and aesthetically pleasing portrait.

Why this happens: When you take a photo from very close up, the lens creates a "wide-angle" effect that distorts the proportions of facial features. The nose appears larger, the face looks wider, and features closer to the camera appear exaggerated. By stepping back several feet and using optical zoom, you maintain the same subject size while using a more natural perspective that preserves facial proportions.
